Competition Rules

Fair play and safety guidelines for all participants

Safety Requirements

  • All robots must have a functioning killswitch accessible from outside the arena
  • Weapons must be completely disabled when not in active competition
  • Safety gear required in pit areas at all times
  • Mandatory safety inspection before each competition

Weight Classes

Antweight 1 lb (454g)
Beetleweight 3 lb (1.36kg)
Middleweight 12 lb (5.44kg)
Heavyweight 250 lb (113kg)

Combat Guidelines

  • Standard match duration: 3 minutes
  • Victory conditions: KO, TKO, or judge's decision
  • No intentional targeting of opponent's electronics
  • Good sportsmanship required at all times

Technical Requirements

  • Maximum voltage: 48V for heavyweight, scaled down for smaller classes
  • Radio frequency coordination required
  • Weapon limitations based on class and safety considerations
  • Dimensional restrictions apply to ensure arena compatibility

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about combat robotics

Costs vary significantly by weight class and complexity. A basic beetleweight (3lb) robot can be built for $200-500, while a competitive heavyweight (250lb) robot can cost $5,000-20,000+. We recommend starting with smaller classes to learn the fundamentals before investing in larger bots.

No formal engineering background is required! Many successful builders started as complete beginners. Our community is very welcoming and experienced builders are always willing to help newcomers. We recommend starting with our workshops and joining our Discord for mentorship.

Safety is our top priority. All competitions are held in reinforced arenas with safety barriers. Strict safety protocols are enforced in pit areas, and all robots undergo thorough safety inspections. While there are inherent risks in building and operating combat robots, following proper safety procedures minimizes danger significantly.

Competitors under 18 must have adult supervision and parental consent. We encourage young builders and have specific safety protocols for underage participants. Many of our most innovative builders started in high school robotics programs.

We typically hold 3-4 major events per year, including our Spring Championship, Summer Showdown, and Fall Tournament. We also organize monthly workshops, building sessions, and smaller meetups throughout the year to keep the community engaged.

Basic hand tools and a small workspace are sufficient for smaller weight classes. Many builders work in garages or basement workshops. For larger robots, access to machining equipment becomes more important, but many makerspaces and community workshops provide shared access to professional tools.
