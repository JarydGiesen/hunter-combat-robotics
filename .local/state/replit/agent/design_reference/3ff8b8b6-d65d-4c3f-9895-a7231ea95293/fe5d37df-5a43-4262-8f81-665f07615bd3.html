Competition Rules

Safety, fairness, and technical standards for all competitors

Weight Classes

Antweight (1 lb)

Maximum weight: 1 pound

Beetleweight (3 lb)

Maximum weight: 3 pounds

Featherweight (30 lb)

Maximum weight: 30 pounds

Heavyweight (250 lb)

Maximum weight: 250 pounds

Safety Requirements

  • All robots must pass safety inspection before competition
  • Emergency stop (E-stop) systems required for all classes
  • Weapon safety locks mandatory during transport and setup
  • Full protective gear required for all participants

Combat Rules

  • 3-minute match duration with 1-minute overtime if needed
  • Victory by knockout, technical knockout, or judge decision
  • No projectiles, entanglement devices, or flame weapons
  • Judges score based on damage, aggression, and control

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about getting started

Do I need prior experience to compete?

Not at all! We welcome builders of all skill levels. Our community is very supportive of newcomers, and we offer mentorship programs to help you get started. Many successful builders started with zero robotics experience.

How much does it cost to build a combat robot?

Costs vary by weight class. Antweights can be built for $100-300, while featherweights typically cost $500-2000. We provide detailed budget guides and parts recommendations for each class.

What tools and workspace do I need?

Basic hand tools can get you started for smaller weight classes. Our partner makerspaces offer access to more advanced equipment like CNC machines and welders for larger robots.

How long does it take to build a robot?

First-time builders typically spend 2-6 months on their robot, depending on complexity and available time. Simple antweights can be completed in weeks, while heavyweight builds may take a year or more.

Are there age restrictions?

Participants under 18 must have adult supervision and parental consent. We have special youth divisions and educational programs to encourage young builders to get involved safely.

What happens if my robot gets damaged?

Damage is part of the sport! Most builders plan for repairs and bring spare parts. Our pit area has basic tools available, and experienced builders often help each other with quick fixes between matches.

Getting Started

Your journey from idea to arena starts here

1

Choose Your Weight Class

Start with antweight (1 lb) or beetleweight (3 lb) for your first robot. These classes are more affordable, easier to build, and perfect for learning the basics.

Antweight (1 lb)

Budget: $100-300 | Build time: 2-6 weeks

Beetleweight (3 lb)

Budget: $200-600 | Build time: 1-3 months

2

Learn the Basics

Familiarize yourself with robot combat fundamentals, safety requirements, and design principles.

  • Read our comprehensive rule book and safety guidelines
  • Watch build tutorials and competition footage
  • Join our Discord community for real-time help and advice
3

Design Your Robot

Plan your robot's design considering weapon type, drive system, and armor configuration.

Weapon Types

Spinners, flippers, hammers, crushers

Drive Systems

Tank drive, mecanum, omni wheels

Armor Materials

Aluminum, steel, titanium, HDPE

4

Build and Test

Start building with basic tools, then test thoroughly before your first competition.

Safety First: Always test weapons in a safe environment and follow all safety protocols during construction.
